# CodeXio
CodexMingle User Guide & Platform Documentation

1. Introduction

What is CodexMingle?

CodexMingle is a collaborative learning and project-development ecosystem designed to help beginners, aspiring developers, and technology enthusiasts learn, build, collaborate, and gain practical experience through community-driven education.

CodexMingle goes beyond traditional online learning by combining structured education with practical projects, mentorship, reputation systems, and career opportunities.

Motto

"Togetherness is Success."

---

Vision

To build an ecosystem where technology learners grow together rather than learn in isolation.

CodexMingle encourages:

- Collaborative learning
- Team projects
- Peer support
- Mentor guidance
- Practical experience
- Career development
- Employment opportunities

Its mission is to bridge the gap between:

Learning → Practice → Collaboration → Employment

---

2. Why CodexMingle Was Created

Many learners struggle with:

- Too many scattered learning resources
- Lack of practical experience
- Limited access to mentors
- Absence of project portfolios
- Difficulty securing their first jobs
- Learning without community support

CodexMingle addresses these problems through four major pillars:

Learning

Structured courses and guided learning paths.

Practice

Assignments, coding exercises, assessments, and projects.

Collaboration

Students work together, contribute to repositories, and participate in team development.

Mentorship

Guidance from instructors and experienced mentors.

Career Opportunities

Recruiters can discover talented learners through their achievements and contributions.

6. Registration & Authentication

Account Creation

Users register using:

- Username
- Full Name
- Email Address
- Phone Number
- Country
- Password

Email Verification

After registration:

1. A verification email is sent.
2. The user clicks the verification link.
3. The account becomes verified.
4. Login access is enabled.

Unverified users cannot access student resources.

---

Student ID Generation

Every student receives a unique ID.

Examples:

CM-2026-0001
CM-2026-0002
CM-2026-0003

These IDs are used for:

- Identification
- Certificates
- Internal tracking

8. Student Reputation System

Each learner has a reputation score that reflects their activity and contribution.

Reputation Formula

score = (
    completed_courses * 10
    + github_contributions * 1
    + assignments_submitted * 2
    + live_attendance * 3
    + mentor_sessions * 5
)

Purpose

- Encourage consistency
- Promote collaboration
- Recognize active learners
- Improve recruiter visibility
- Build credibility

12. Meet Your Mentor System

Available exclusively to Live Cohort students.

Purpose

Provide personalized support while maintaining scalability and preventing mentor burnout.

Workflow

1. Student requests a session.
2. Request is reviewed.
3. Mentor evaluates the need.
4. Session approval is granted.
5. Meeting is scheduled.

Session Limits

To ensure fairness, students have limited mentor sessions.

Benefits:

- Equal access
- Sustainable mentorship
- Better scalability
